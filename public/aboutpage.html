What are Pokemons?

Pokémon (or Pocket Monsters) are fictional creatures that inhabit the fictional Pokémon World. The designs for the multitude of species can draw inspiration from anything, such as animals, plants, and legendary creatures.

Pokémon Types

Normal

Balanced, versatile, few strengths but few weaknesses.

Fire

Strong against Grass, Bug, Ice, and Steel; often powerful attackers.

Water

Balanced and common; effective against Fire, Rock, and Ground.

Grass

Good against Water, Rock, and Ground; often status-inducing.

Electric

Fast and strong against Water and Flying.

Ice

Strong vs. Flying, Ground, Grass, Dragon; often fragile but hard-hitting.

Fighting

Strong vs. Normal, Dark, Rock, Steel, Ice; physical powerhouses.

Poison

Strong against Grass and Fairy; often status specialists.

Ground

Effective against Fire, Electric, Poison, Rock, Steel.

Flying

Good against Grass, Bug, Fighting; often fast and evasive.

Psychic

Strong against Fighting and Poison; often special attackers.

Bug

Strong against Psychic, Grass, Dark; usually underdogs but strategic.

Rock

Good vs. Flying, Fire, Bug, Ice; defensively tough.

Ghost

Immune to Normal & Fighting; great against Psychic & Ghost.

Dragon

Strong and legendary; weak mainly to Fairy, Ice, and Dragon.

Dark

Great vs. Psychic & Ghost; tricksters with sneaky tactics.

Steel

Excellent defenses; strong against Ice, Rock, Fairy.

Fairy

Introduced in Gen 6; strong against Dragon, Dark, Fighting.

Different Kinds of Pokédex

Kanto Pokédex

The first Pokédex, recording the original 151 Pokémon.

Johto Pokédex

Expanded with new evolutions and forms from Gen 2.

Hoenn Pokédex

Features region-specific species, later split into Normal/National Dex.

Sinnoh Pokédex

Introduced evolutions for many older Pokémon.

Unova Pokédex

Initially only Unova Pokémon; later expanded to National Dex.

Kalos Pokédex

Divided into Central, Coastal, and Mountain Dexes.

Alola Pokédex

Includes regional "Alolan forms" of classic Pokémon.

Galar Pokédex

Focuses on regional Pokémon and "Galarian forms."

Paldea Pokédex

Latest, includes regional forms and Terastal phenomenon entries.

Satoshi Tajiri

Satoshi Tajiri (Japanese: 田尻 智, Hepburn: Tajiri Satoshi, born August 28, 1965) is a Japanese video game designer and director who is the creator of the Pokémon franchise and the co-founder and president of video game developer Game Freak.

A fan of arcade games in his youth, Tajiri wrote for and edited his own video gaming fanzine Game Freak with Ken Sugimori, before evolving it into a development company of the same name. Tajiri claims that the joining of two Game Boys via a link cable inspired him to create a game which embodied the collection and companionship of his childhood hobby, insect collecting. The game, which became Pokémon Red and Pokémon Green, took six years to complete and went on to spark a multibillion-dollar franchise which reinvigorated Nintendo's handheld gaming scene.

Tajiri continued to work as director for the Pokémon series until the development of Pokémon Ruby and Sapphire, when he changed his role to executive producer, which he holds to this day.
